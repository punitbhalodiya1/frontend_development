Hello User,

We are glad to inform you that we will soon be solving the issues related to your WhatsApp ID and Phone number mismatch. (Cases where operator changed or extended a mobile number after the user registered on WhatsApp)

Here's a summary of what's going to happen :

Problem:

Let's say, a user's old phone number was '1234' and the user is registered on WhatsApp with the same number.

Later, the phone number was updated by the operator to '91234', however, their WhatsApp number still remains '1234'. Here are the below cases that take place -

Current implementation:
  1. Outgoing message -
    • Business opts in '91234'
    • Business sends template message or session message to '91234'
    • Template message gets delivered to '1234', and the business makes a note of it from the sent event and maintains the mapping of 91234 & 1234.
  2. Incoming message -
    • Business receives session message from '1234'
    • Business has to check the mapping and find out that '1234' is linked to '91234'

Resolution:

Gupshup WhatsApp Business API solution will now enable the below process -

  1. Outgoing message -
    • Business opts in '91234'
    • Business sends template message to '91234'
    • if GS gets wa_id as '1234', template message gets delivered to '1234'
    • GS stores mapping of '1234' to '91234' for session message
    • GS sends a number mismatch event if applicable (From Jan 13, 2022) for ISV to make changes in their database if needed. [Message will flow as usual]
    • ISV may replace '91234' to '1234' once they get a number mismatch event and mark '1234' as an opt-in, to send template message in future
  2. Incoming message -
    • Business receives session message from '1234'
    • If a business has integrated the mismatch event then they will not have to perform the mapping check.

The impacting changes are scheduled to go live from 13th January 2022.
