COMING SOON: Sunset of OBO (traditional) method and On-Premise hosting options

As Meta sunsets the on-premise hosting option soon, Gupshup will also disable on-premise hosting soon as well as the OBO onboarding method. After the change, all WABA onboarding will be mandatory through the Embedded Signup on Cloud API only (Exception: Regions who do not support Cloud API hosting)

Note: Existing WABAs on OBO or on On-prem will continue to work till further notice

COMING SOON: Other BSP to Gupshup Migration flow changes

Embedded signup migration flow will soon see some improvements, where the migration will now take place entirely through the Embedded Signup flow, without the Gupshup interface at the end asking for WABA ID and Phone number mapping.

COMING SOON: WhatsApp fee deduction based on delivered messages

Gupshup will soon transition to charging WhatsApp fees on delivery (to the user) rather than sent. Note: Gupshup fee will continue to be charged on sent only.

NEW POLICY: Unused commission lapse policy announced

Recently we informed partners about the new policy of consuming commissions, to avoid making them lapse. To avoid it, partners must consume their unconsumed commissions from and before March 2023 and must complete the activity before April 2024.

RECOMMENDED: Send Message Endpoint v2

We recommend our partners start using Send Message Endpoints v2 - i.e https://api.gupshup.io/wa/api/v1/msg and avoid using v1 i.e https://api.gupshup.io/sm/api/v1/msg to ensure a smooth messaging experience.

RECOMMENDED: Adapt to the upcoming opt-in service changes

While we might soon be getting rid of the opt-in service altogether for partners, we recommend they avoid using the opt-in and opt-out registration service even today, as we are not checking opt-in and opt-out anymore. Partners must also continue using the ‘API to disable automated opt-in messages’ if they want to avoid sending automated opt-in messages from Gupshup. Refer docs

REMINDER: Partner Linking Methods update

The recommended method for linking partners should be through the Partner API, or by utilizing the Gupshup Self-Serve UI. Please note, that auto-linking via the Callback URL pattern is no longer supported.

RECOMMENDED: Use Upgraded Partner Documentation

We recommend our partners to refer Partner Documentation now available on docs.gupshup.io as the old documentation on partner.gupshup.io will not be maintained.

Meta Updates

Template Pacing for Utility Templates from April 30:

Template pacing will be expanded to utility messages to improve engagement and user experiences. If a utility template for an account gets paused due to user feedback, Meta will pace the new utility templates that are created for the next 7 days

Simplified Scaling without BM verification and DN review :

Businesses currently capped at 2 phone numbers and 250 daily conversations may access up to 20 phone numbers per business manager and 1,000+ daily business-initiated conversations by demonstrating a record of at least 1,000 high-quality business-initiated conversations within 30 consecutive days, without BM verification or DN review.

Extension to non-compliant authentication templates till May 31, 2024:

Last year, Meta introduced new guidelines for authentication message templates for new messages. While previous communication informed us that legacy auth templates that do not meet these updated guidelines would be rejected on April 1, 2024, Meta has now extended the deadline till May 31, 2024. They will update the status of any authentication template that does not meet the authentication template guidelines to 'rejected' after this point.

New international authentication rates and launch of authentication in India:

Starting June 1, 2024, Meta is introducing a new authentication-international rate which will apply in Indonesia on June 1, 2024. Authentication templates will also go live in India on July 1, 2024 (allowing you to send auth templates to Indian users) along with the authentication-international rate. Authentication-international rates apply depending on the phone number to which you are sending your authentication message and where your business is based.
