Amazonian Volunteers Clean Up Local Seattle Parks

Over the weekend, 500 Amazonians volunteered in a citywide park cleanup in partnership with Seattle Parks Foundation, the Mayor’s Office, and the Seattle Department of Parks and Recreation. In five parks across the city, Amazonians removed hundreds of pounds of invasive weeds, fertilized urban forests with mulch, and cleaned up litter along the waterfront. In total, Amazonians save the city over $45,000 in taxpayer dollars for their work, and helped contribute to Seattle’s goal of establishing 30 percent tree canopy cover in the city by 2037.

Back-to-School Block Party at Amazon Fresh in Factoria Featuring Ciara

Amazon and Ascend Hospitality Group (AHG) teamed up with Grammy-award winning artist Ciara to support more than 500 low-income students with new backpacks filled with school supplies! Students from across the Puget Sound region came to the Amazon Fresh store to meet Ciara, design their own backpacks, play games, dance, and enjoy lunch. Along with Ciara, 30 Amazonians, Rep. Tana Senn and Bellevue Deputy Mayor Jared Nieuwenhuis helped to distribute the backpacks.
