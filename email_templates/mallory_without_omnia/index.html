Why you need an AED?

Time is of the essence: Every minute without CPR decreases the survival rate from sudden cardiac arrest by 7-10%.

Bystanders can make a difference: Early intervention by trained bystanders can significantly improve the chances of survival.

Be ready to respond: Having readily available AEDs and trained personnel can save lives in critical moments.

OSHA : Saving Sudden Cardiac Arrest Victims in the Workplace

Mallory Safety and Supply supports the American Heart Association whose Chain of Survival Guidelines are integral to the ZOLL AED operation process:

You don't need to be a trained medical professional to help save a life. As a layperson, having an awareness of SCA and knowing how to respond can make all the difference between life and death. If a layperson witnesses sudden cardiac arrest, they should follow these six links of the Chain of Survival:

Activation of the emergency response system

Immediate high-quality CPR

Rapid defibrillation

Basic and advanced emergency medical services

Advanced life support and post-arrest care
